# Final Recommendation

The model points to footfall as the strongest sales driver, followed by marketing_spend. Leadership should focus first on the factors that are large and statistically useful in the multiple regression, not just the variables that look good in a simple one-variable model.

Recommended actions:
- Protect and improve the drivers with the strongest useful coefficients.
- Use store-level residuals to review locations that beat or miss the model by a large amount.
- Do not over-interpret avg_discount_pct because its p-value is not strong in the final model.

Regression shows association. It does not prove that changing one variable will automatically cause sales to move by the coefficient amount. Marketing, footfall, staffing, and inventory can influence each other, and some local store conditions are not in the file.

My recommendation is to use the final model for prioritization, then validate actions with controlled pilots or before-and-after tracking.
